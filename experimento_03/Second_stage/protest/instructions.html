The incentives are the following:

  • They pay a cost from protesting, which (perhaps, but is not necessary) diminishes with the number of protesters
  • If enough protest, the regime switches. The switching function may be discrete (a threshold rule) or more continuous. If the regime switches, another regime is randomly selected.
  • After the protest decision, there is a regime in place (either the same one if there was no switch, or whichever one was switched to). Call this regime n_hat. I have several versions of what the payoffs to this regime switch are. In all these versions, someone will be assigned a payoff that is decreasing in the value of the column chosen (between 1 and m) from row n_hat.
